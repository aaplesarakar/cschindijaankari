Frequently Asked Questions - FAQs

What is the course duration?

The duration of the course includes 30 hours of online training and assessment that comprises:
• 20 Days, 5 Days a week (1.5 Hours per day)
• Complimentary book on “3D Printing Made Simple” will be delivered to all participants.

What is the course fee?

Course fee is Rs 2,183.

What is the commission for VLE?

VLE may charge as per the local market value from the candidate.

What is approximate cost of 3D printer which needs to be procured later?

Starting price of 3D printer is approximately Rs. 12,000. It depends on model to model.

How VLE’s can arrange license version of CAD application software?

CSC team will arrange student version software for all registered candidates.

What should be the basic configuration of computer?

Basic configuration required for the course are:
• Operating System: Microsoft Windows 10 (64-bit only), 8.1 (64-bit only), or 7 SP1 (64-bit only)
• Processor: i3 (2.0+ GHz recommended)
• Memory: 4 GB (8GB recommended) Disk space: 6.0 GB
• Display: 1920 x 1080 resolution with True Color

Do VLE need to have some environmental permission for this printing?

Yes, it depends on the type of material used for manufacturing products using 3D printing.

How a candidate will get study material/practice software?

CSC team will share course content and CAD software downloadable link at candidate registered e-mail id.

What is mode of training for the course?

Online trainer-led training is administered in the course where 3D printer takes digital input in the form of Computer Aided Design (CAD) model and creates solid, three dimensional parts through an additive, layer by layer process. Before manufacturing a solid three-dimensional part, the digital file of that part is designed using CAD tools.

What is the minimum qualification required for enrolling in the course?

Minimum qualification required to for a student to join the course is that he/she should be 12th pass.

Who will certify the student on course completion?

After completion of the online course, CSC Academy and Ecole Supérieure Robert de Sorbon®️ Institute certificate will be awarded to participants followed by an online assessment.

Who is this course for?

The integrated course on Computer-Aided Design and 3D Printing is designed for beginners and no prior knowledge in 3D Printing and design is necessary for the same.
School kids, engineers, designers, hobbyists, artists and anyone looking to learn how to design their own models for 3D printing can join the course.
All those who want to enhance their skill set and find a potential business opportunity in the space of designing and 3D printing can be benefitted through this course.

What are learning outcomes of this course?

Learning outcomes for students through this course are:
• Sketching and Modelling
• Faster design using synchronous technology
• Surfacing of solid models as per industrial standard
• Drafting as per Industrial standard
• Modelling of 3D Frames as per different standards
• Understand the design aspects of the 3D printable file
• Know the process of 3D printer
• Be able to understand the FDM methodology
• Know about 3D printing case studies
• Know the process of 3D printer

How much disk space is needed to download and install CAD software?

CAD content and practice software requires approximately 20GB free space in hard disk.
